About Me

Hi, welcome to my web-page! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Chemistry (Theoretical and Computational Track) at UC San Diego, where my Ph.D. studies are supported in part by the awarded Fulbright-García Robles Scholarship. I currently work at the Amaro Lab specializing in computational approaches for drug discovery. My work is driven by a motivation to design safer and more effective therapeutics for conditions that historically lack adequate treatments. For instance, during my Master’s research I proposed a derivative of a commercial antipsychotic aimed at reducing the cardiometabolic side effects often associated with schizophrenia medications.

My current research integrates Deep Learning (DL) and Molecular Simulations to accelerate antiviral discovery. I develop new graph-based neural networks (GNNs), specifically message-passing neural networks (MPNNs) for molecular and atom-level predictions, with a focus on identifying and optimizing inhibitors of the SARS-CoV-2 NSP13 helicase. I am also interested in large-scale exploration of chemical space, both to understand its diversity and to adapt DL models for broader predictive power.

Beyond machine learning, I apply enhanced molecular simulations, including metadynamics, gaussian-accelerated dynamics, and free-energy calculations, to probe biomolecular mechanisms with high accuracy. My Ph.D. thesis focuses on understanding how the SARS-CoV-2 helicase translocates RNA using nucleoside triphosphates (NTPs), and on developing strategies to inhibit this essential viral process.

Earlier in my career, I conducted in vitro studies on plant viruses, exploring how icosahedral viruses can be engineered as nanocapsids for targeted drug delivery.
